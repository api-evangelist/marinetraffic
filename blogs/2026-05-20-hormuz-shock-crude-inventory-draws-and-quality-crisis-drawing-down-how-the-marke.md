Surplus buffers are depleted, deficits of 2.6 Mbd loom in May, and a quality mismatch compounds the volumetric crisis in global crude markets. The crude market's initial buffers - floating storage, oil in transit, and surplus barrels - have been exhausted by the Strait of Hormuz disruption, leaving accelerating commercial inventory draws, a ~2.6 Mbd May deficit, and a structural quality mismatch as the final defences against a tightening global supply crunch.
